I'm a master's student in Electrical Engineering at Northwestern University and a graduate researcher in the AIMP-Lab. My research sits at the intersection of generative models and medical image analysis.

Before Northwestern, I completed a First-Class B.Eng. in Electronic and Electrical Engineering at the University of Birmingham. I'm especially interested in making generative image translation reliable enough for clinical use — preserving absolute physical quantities such as SUV, not just visual fidelity.

02Research

Research focus

A few directions my work has centered on. Feel free to edit or add more.

01

CT-Free PET Attenuation Correction

Diffusion-based generative methods for CT-free PET attenuation correction, with a focus on preserving absolute SUV quantification through explicit, physically-grounded feature guidance (Dual-Stream Gated Skip + Brownian Bridge Diffusion).

02

Missing-Modality Medical Imaging

Robust multi-modal frameworks that compensate for missing MRI modalities, enabling reliable diagnosis such as liver fibrosis staging from non-contrast T1WI / T2WI / DWI.

03

Generative Image-to-Image Translation

A taxonomy of diffusion-based image-to-image translation — trajectory parameterization, condition injection, and the efficiency–fidelity trade-off — read through the lens of quantitative medical imaging.
